Pedagogical Approach

Scenarios before theory. Frameworks as interpretive lenses, not prescriptions. Every concept gets a practitioner story and a "so what" before the academic framing arrives. Executives learn by pattern recognition, not memorisation.

"The question is never 'what is platform strategy?' The question is 'given this specific competitive landscape, what would you do differently — and why?'"

Teaching Philosophy

Core Modules

The Platform Paradox — Why platforms must destroy value to create it. The two-sided market problem in practice.
Network Effects Architecture — Direct, indirect, cross-side. Why indirect network effects are the most powerful and the most misunderstood.
The Lock-in Stack — Data, integration, habit, social proof. How mechanisms combine — and why orchestration quality is the variable that matters.
Multi-Homing & Churn — When users stay on multiple platforms and what that means for growth strategy.
Platform Defence & Attack — Incumbent advantages and how challengers find the gap the leader cannot close.
The Agent Economy Shift — How AI agents are restructuring platform power dynamics right now.
