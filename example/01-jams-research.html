Platform Entrenchment & Switching Cost Dynamics

How digital platforms achieve durable user lock-in — and what determines the speed and depth of that entrenchment over time.

Author: Muralikrishnan B. · ISB EFPM Research Programme

Status: Conceptual Model v1 · Classification: Research

Platform Economics Switching Costs S-D Logic Orchestration JAMS 2026

Core Argument

Platform entrenchment is not a single mechanism — it is a compounding system. Data, habits, integrations, and social costs reinforce each other, creating depth that a new entrant cannot shortcut regardless of price or UX quality.

"The moat is not any single switching cost — it is the time-compounded, feedback-reinforced depth that a challenger cannot shortcut."

Hermes Unbound · Chapter 3 · Draft

Mechanism Architecture

Data Moat — Behavioural history compounds into personalised output no new user can replicate without equivalent tenure.
Integration Stack — API connections span multiple systems. Switching means rebuilding an ecosystem, not just a workflow.
Reputation Capital — Reviews and ratings are non-transferable assets. Switching erases credibility built over years.
Habit Architecture — Trigger → routine → reward loops make platform use invisible infrastructure, not a deliberate choice.
Social Proof Cascade — Network effects make switching a collective decision, not an individual one.
Cognitive Lock-in — Learning investment becomes identity. Leaving feels like skill depreciation.
